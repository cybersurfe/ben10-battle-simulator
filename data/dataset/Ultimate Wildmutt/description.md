Appearance
----------


Ultimate Wildmutt is significantly larger and more muscular than Wildmutt. His fur is a maroon-red color and he has four huge gray/white spikes running down his back that overlap. These spikes start out as a shark-like fin at the head and rises almost into an arc over the back, tapering near the rump. He also has a long tail with a curved horned tip, resembling a scorpion stinger. His lips are black, like Wildmutt in the original series, and he has white claws.


Ultimate Wildmutt wears the evolved [Ultimatrix](/wiki/Ultimatrix_(Original) "Ultimatrix (Original)") symbol on his chest.

--------------------

Much like his unevolved counterpart, Ultimate Wildmutt has incredible senses of smell and hearing.


Ultimate Wildmutt possesses enhanced strength. Technically speaking, he is physically stronger than regular Vulpimancers and [Merlinisapiens](/wiki/Merlinisapien "Merlinisapien").


Unlike regular Wildmutt, Ultimate Wildmutt has the ability to speak other languages than the Vulpimancer language. 


Ultimate Wildmutt is more agile than Wildmutt.


Ultimate Wildmutt has sharp claws and teeth, which can be used for close range combat. He also has a scorpion-like tail with a sharp tip.[[merch 1]](#cite_note-2)



Weaknesses
----------


Like his base form, Ultimate Wildmutt cannot see and his vision is only made up of a dull 3-D mapping.

Trivia
------


* Ultimate Wildmutt is the last [Ultimate form](/wiki/Ultimate_Forms "Ultimate Forms") used by [Ben](/wiki/Ben_Tennyson_(Classic) "Ben Tennyson (Classic)") onscreen in **Ultimate Alien**.
* Ultimate Wildmutt's sentient counterpart did not appear in *[The Ultimate Sacrifice](/wiki/The_Ultimate_Sacrifice "The Ultimate Sacrifice")*, even though the synopsis said that all of the [Sentient Ultimate Forms](/wiki/Ultimate_Forms#Sentient_Forms "Ultimate Forms") want freedom.
* Ultimate Wildmutt was the first [Ultimate Form](/wiki/Ultimate_Forms "Ultimate Forms") to be hinted on the DVD cover of **[Ultimate Alien](/wiki/Ben_10:_Ultimate_Alien "Ben 10: Ultimate Alien")** named *The Wild Truth*, where his view is shown, with Ultimate Wildmutt behind Ben.
* [Derrick J. Wyatt](/wiki/Derrick_J._Wyatt "Derrick J. Wyatt") did concept art for Ultimate Wildmutt as part of freelance work, who used inspiration from looking at dinosaurs. However, none of his designs were used for the final version.[[DJW 1]](#cite_note-FreelanceArt-3)[[DJW 2]](#cite_note-4)
	+ One of Ultimate Wildmutt's unused concept art by Jon Suzuki would later be repurposed as the basis for [Solid Plugg](/wiki/Solid_Plugg "Solid Plugg")'s design.[[DJW 3]](#cite_note-5)
	+ Strangely, Ultimate Wildmutt's final design bears a striking resemblance to the *Godzilla* franchise monster, Titanosaurus, with a red body with a white-yellow frill running down its back and onto its tail.
* The **Omniverse** crew did not discuss bringing Ultimate Wildmutt back.[[DJW 1]](#cite_note-FreelanceArt-3)
